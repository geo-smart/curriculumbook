Model Performance#

To measure a fit performance, we will measure several metrics.

  1. Standard Error

The variance of tge errors is reduced by fitting the data (scatter points) against the model. The variance is:

\(\text{Var}(x) = 1/n \sum_{i=1}^n (x_i-\hat{x})^2\),

where \(\hat{x}\) is the mean of \(x\). When fitting the regression, we predict the values \(x_{pred}\). The residuals are the differences between the data and the predicted values: \(e = x - x_{pred} \). \(R^2\) or coefficient of determination is:

\(R^2 = 1 - \text{Var}(x-x_{pred}) /\text{Var}(x) = 1 - \text{Var}(e) /\text{Var}(x) \) The smaller the error, the “better” the fit (we will discuss later that a fit can be too good!), the closter \(R^2\) is to one.
